# After an Earthquake: Safety, Inspection, and Recovery

The danger isn't over when the shaking stops. **Aftershocks, fires, utility damage, and unstable structures** cause more casualties than the main shock in many earthquakes.

## Immediate Actions (First Minutes to Hours)

### 1. **Expect Aftershocks**
- **Rule**: Aftershocks can be as large as the main shock (rare) or nearly as large
- **Action**: Stay in safe location; **Drop, Cover, Hold On** for each aftershock
- **Duration**: Can continue for days, weeks, months; largest usually within first 24–48 hours

### 2. **Check for Injuries**
- **First**: Check yourself, then others
- **Do not move seriously injured** unless in immediate danger
- **Call emergency services** for life-threatening injuries (text if lines busy)

### 3. **Check for Hazards — The "Four Killers"**

| Hazard | Detection | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Gas leak** | Smell rotten eggs; hissing sound | **Evacuate immediately**; shut off gas at meter if safe; **no flames/sparks** |
| **Electrical** | Sparks, frayed wires, burning smell | Shut off at main breaker if safe; don't touch wet electrical |
| **Water** | Flooding, rushing water sound | Shut off at main valve; prevents further damage + electrocution |
| **Structural** | New cracks, bulging walls, leaning, separated joints | **Evacuate**; do not re-enter until inspected |

### 4. **Fire — Most Common Post-EQ Killer**
- Small fires: use fire extinguisher (PASS: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep)
- Large fires: **evacuate**; close doors to contain; call fire department
- **Do not use water on electrical/gas fires**

## Building Safety Assessment (ATC-20 Framework)

### Red / Yellow / Green Tagging

| Tag | Meaning | Action |
|-----|---------|--------|
| **GREEN (Inspected)** | No apparent structural hazard | Safe to occupy; monitor for changes |
| **YELLOW (Restricted Use)** | Damage limits safe occupancy | **Do not occupy** except for essential retrieval; entry at own risk |
| **RED (Unsafe)** | Severe damage; collapse risk | **Do not enter**; demolition likely |

### Quick Visual Inspection Triggers (Red Flags for YELLOW/RED)

| Exterior | Interior | Foundation |
|----------|----------|------------|
| Leaning walls/chimneys | New cracks >1/4" (6mm) | New cracks in foundation |
| Separation at corners | Sticking doors/windows (frame rack) | Soil movement/liquefaction |
| Bulging walls | Ceiling/wall separation | House off foundation |
| Falling parapades | Sloping floors | Cracked perimeter grade beam |
| Diagonal stair-step cracks | Buckled/fallen plaster | Piers/columns damaged |

## Utilities Restoration

| Utility | When to Restore | Who |
|---------|-----------------|-----|
| **Gas** | **Only by utility company** after inspection | Gas company |
| **Electric** | After structural inspection; no water contact | Electrician / utility |
| **Water** | After pressure test; boil advisory likely | Water dept. |
| **Sewer** | Verify no line breaks before use | Sanitation dept. |

## Shelter and Basic Needs

| Need | Source | Duration |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Emergency shelter** | Schools, community centers, pagodas, open spaces | Days–weeks |
| **Water** | Stored, bottled, boiled (1 min rolling boil), purification tablets | 1 gal/person/day |
| **Food** | Emergency kit, distribution points, community kitchens | Non-perishable first |
| **Medical** | First aid stations, field hospitals, functioning hospitals | Triage: life-threatening first |
| **Information** | Battery/crank radio, MEC alerts, community networks | Official sources only |

## Myanmar-Specific After-Actions

| Context | Guidance |
|---------|----------|
| **Traditional housing (bamboo/timber)** | May appear intact but joints loosened — inspect connections before re-occupying |
| **Monastery/pagoda** | Do not enter until inspected by engineer; spires/ornaments are falling hazards |
| **River/coastal communities** | **Move to high ground immediately** — tsunami risk from local or distant faults |
| **Yangon high-rises** | Expect elevator outage; check for structural damage on lower floors (soft story) |
| **Community response** | Leverage local CBOs, monastic networks, Red Cross Myanmar for distribution |
| **Aftershock preparedness** | Sleep outdoors if structure doubtful; keep shoes/flashlight by bed |

## Recovery Timeline (Typical)

| Phase | Duration | Key Activities |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| **Emergency** | 0–72 hrs | Rescue, medical, shelter, water, safety assessment |
| **Stabilization** | 1–4 weeks | Utility restore, temporary housing, debris removal |
| **Recovery** | 1–12 months | Repair/rebuild, permanent housing, livelihood restoration |
| **Resilience** | 1–5+ years | Code upgrades, retrofit programs, community planning |
