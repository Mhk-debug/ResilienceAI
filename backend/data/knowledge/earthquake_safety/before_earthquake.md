# Before an Earthquake: Preparedness and Mitigation

The most effective earthquake safety actions happen **before** the shaking starts. Preparation reduces injuries, saves lives, and speeds recovery.

## 1. Secure Your Space (Non-Structural Mitigation)

| Priority | Action | Why It Matters |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| **High** | Anchor tall furniture (bookcases, cabinets) to wall studs | Prevents tip-over — leading cause of indoor injuries |
| **High** | Secure water heater with metal straps | Prevents gas leak, fire, water loss |
| **High** | Install flexible gas/water connectors | Reduces post-EQ fire risk |
| **Medium** | Put heavy items on lower shelves | Reduces falling hazards |
| **Medium** | Secure hanging plants, mirrors, pictures with closed hooks | Prevents shattering/falling |
| **Medium** | Latch cabinet doors | Keeps contents from becoming projectiles |
| **Low** | Store flammable liquids away from ignition sources | Reduces post-EQ fire |

## 2. Create an Emergency Plan

**Family Communication Plan:**
- Out-of-area contact (everyone texts/calls same person)
- Meeting place near home + meeting place outside neighborhood
- School/work emergency procedures for each member
- Pet plan (most shelters don't accept animals)

**Home-Specific:**
- Know how to shut off gas, water, electricity
- Keep flashlight + sturdy shoes by each bed
- Practice "Drop, Cover, Hold On" — 2x/year minimum

## 3. Build an Emergency Kit (72-Hour Minimum)

| Category | Items |
|----------|-------|
| **Water** | 1 gal/person/day × 3 days = 3 gal/person |
| **Food** | Non-perishable, no-cook (canned, bars, peanut butter) × 3 days |
| **Medical** | First aid kit, 7-day meds, glasses/contacts, hygiene items |
| **Tools** | Flashlight + extra batteries, multi-tool, whistle, dust masks (N95) |
| **Documents** | Copies of IDs, insurance, medical info, cash (small bills) |
| **Special** | Baby supplies, pet food, elderly needs |

**Location**: Keep at home, in car, and at work/school.

## 3. Structural Preparedness (Homeowner Actions)

| Action | When | Cost | Effectiveness |
|--------|------|------|---------------|
| **Foundation bolting** (anchor bolts, cripple wall bracing) | Pre-1980 wood frame | $3,000–7,000 | Prevents house sliding off foundation |
| **Cripple wall bracing** | Pre-1940 wood frame | $2,000–5,000 | Prevents wall collapse |
| **Chimney bracing/removal** | Unreinforced masonry chimney | $1,500–4,000 | Prevents chimney collapse into house |
| **Mobile home tie-downs** | All manufactured homes | $2,000–4,000 | Prevents rolling off foundation |
| **Gas shutoff valve** (automatic seismic) | All homes with gas | $300–600 | Prevents post-EQ fires |

## 4. Community Preparedness

- **Register for ShakeAlert/Earthquake Early Warning** (Myanmar: Myanmar Earthquake Committee alerts)
- **Participate in Great ShakeOut drill** (annually, 3rd Thursday Oct)
- **Know your neighborhood** — identify vulnerable neighbors, CERT teams
- **Insurance** — Standard policies exclude earthquake; buy separate EQ policy or endorsement

## Myanmar-Specific Before-Actions

| Action | Local Context |
|--------|---------------|
| **Register for MEC alerts** | Myanmar Earthquake Committee (MEC) provides early warning via app/SMS |
| **Know your local pagoda/community center** | Often designated evacuation points |
| **Store water in traditional clay jars** | Cultural practice; ensures supply if municipal fails |
| **Secure Buddha images/shrines** | Heavy religious items are common falling hazards |
| **Bamboo/timber house bracing** | Traditional construction — add diagonal bracing, anchor posts |
