# During an Earthquake: Immediate Life-Safety Actions

**The single most important action: DROP, COVER, AND HOLD ON.**

## The Protocol (Seconds 0–60+)

| Step | Action | Duration | Details |
|------|--------|----------|---------|
| **1. DROP** | Get on hands and knees immediately | Instant | Prevents being knocked down; allows crawling to cover |
| **2. COVER** | Cover head/neck with one arm; get under sturdy table/desk | Instant | If no table: next to interior wall, away from windows |
| **3. HOLD ON** | Hold onto table leg with other arm; move with table if it shifts | **Until shaking stops** | Shaking may last 10–60+ seconds; aftershocks follow |

## Location-Specific Guidance

| Location | Best Action | Avoid |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| **Indoors — at desk/table** | Drop, Cover, Hold On under table | Running outside; doorway (myth) |
| **Indoors — no table** | Drop next to interior wall; cover head/neck | Windows, exterior walls, tall furniture |
| **In bed** | Stay in bed; cover head with pillow | Running across room |
| **High-rise** | Drop, Cover, Hold On; avoid windows; don't use elevators | Evacuating during shaking |
| **Outdoors** | Move to open area; drop, cover, hold on | Buildings, trees, power lines, overpasses |
| **Driving** | Pull over safely; stop; set brake; stay in vehicle | Bridges, overpasses, tunnels, power lines |
| **Theater/Stadium** | Stay in seat; protect head/neck; don't rush exits | Running for exits |
| **Near coast** | Drop, Cover, Hold On; **then move to high ground immediately** (tsunami risk) | Waiting for official warning |

## What NOT to Do (Common Myths)

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| "Stand in a doorway" | Modern doorways are no stronger; door swings can injure |
| "Run outside" | Falling façade/glass kills more than building collapse |
| "Triangle of Life" (curl next to furniture) | **Discredited** — increases injury risk; not endorsed by any major agency |
| "Stand next to heavy furniture" | Furniture can shift/collapse; cover is better |
| "Call 911 immediately" | Lines will be jammed; **text instead**; call only for life-threatening emergencies |

## If You Are Trapped

1. **Stay calm** — conserve energy and air
2. **Cover mouth/nose** with cloth (dust protection)
3. **Tap on pipe/wall** — 3 taps every few minutes (rescuers listen)
4. **Use whistle** if you have one
5. **Do not light matches/lighters** — gas leak risk
6. **Do not move debris** unless it's small and safe — secondary collapse risk

## Myanmar-Specific During-Actions

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **In traditional bamboo/timber house** | Drop, Cover, Hold On under sturdy table; these houses may sway but rarely collapse completely |
| **In unreinforced brick/masonry building** | Get under table; these buildings are highest collapse risk |
| **Near pagoda/temple** | Move away — tall spires, heavy ornamentation fall |
| **On riverbank/delta (Yangon)** | After shaking: **immediately move inland/up** — liquefaction and tsunami risk |
| **Driving in Yangon/Mandalay traffic** | Stay in vehicle; roads may crack, bridges may fail |
